# Designs
# 🎨 DecodeLabs UI/UX Internship - Project 1: User Persona & Empathy Map

This repository contains the foundational UX Research and Problem Space Exploration framework for a fitness application targeting 'Time-Poor' office professionals.

---

## 📌 Problem Statement
Office workers who spend 40+ hours a week sitting at a desk suffer from severe physical friction points (like chronic back pain and poor posture) alongside high mental fatigue. While they desperately need relief, they lack the time for long gym commutes or intensive 45-minute workout routines.

---

## 👥 1. User Persona: Kamran Ahmed
* **Age & Profile:** 32 Years Old | Senior Software Engineer (Desk Job)
* **The Core Shift:** His primary goal is **Mental Clarity** and **Pain Relief** from long sitting hours, *not* vanity, bodybuilding, or dramatic weight loss.
* **Friction Points:** Gyms are too far (wasting time in traffic), existing fitness apps demand too much time, and over-gamified apps with constant loud notifications cause mental irritation.

---

## 🗺️ 2. The Empathy Map (4 Quadrants)
* **THINK & FEEL:** 
  * *"Why am I always so exhausted?"*
  * *"My lower back is killing me from sitting at this computer all day."*
* **SEE:** 
  * Sees his colleagues glued to their chairs for 9-to-5 stretches.
  * Sees endless pending tasks and deadlines piling up on his desktop screen.
* **HEAR:** 
  * Hears online fitness influencers shouting: *"Go to the gym for 1 hour every day!"*—which feels completely impossible for his lifestyle.
  * Hears constant reminders from his manager about upcoming project deadlines.
* **SAY & DO:** 
  * **Says:** *"I literally have zero time to focus on my fitness or health."*
  * **Does:** Whenever he gets a small 5-minute break from work, he ends up mindlessly scrolling through social media reels instead of standing up to stretch.

---

## 💡 3. The Solution Strategy: Desk-Bound Micro-Movements
Instead of forcing the user to leave the office, the application focuses on bringing health directly to the desk through:
* **5-Minute Activity Windows:** Short, actionable breaks designed to fit right inside small gaps in the workday.
* **Seated Stretches:** Graphical, easy-to-follow posture correction exercises that can be performed entirely while sitting in an office chair.
